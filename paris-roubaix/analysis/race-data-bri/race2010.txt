      <h1><b>2010 Paris - Roubaix</b></h1>
      <h3><b>108th edition: Sunday, April 11, 2010</b></h3>
    </div>

                        <p>259 km with 27 sectors of cobbles totaling 52.9 km</p>
                        <p>Paris-Roubaix <a href="paris-roubaix-index.html">podium history</a> | <a href="pr2009.html">2009 edition</a> | <a href="pr2011.html">2011 edition</a><br />
<a href="#results">Results</a> | <a href="#pictures">Photos from the race</a> | <a href="#map"> route map</a> | <a href="#cobbles">list of cobbled sectors</a></p>
                        <p><img src="../../images-all/annual-images/images09/PR1.jpg" alt="" height="398" width="600" border="0" /></p>
                        <p><strong>The Race:</strong> What an athlete! Fabian Cancellara won today's Paris-Roubaix and did both the 2010 Flanders-Roubaix double and also won his second Paris-Roubaix (he won in 2006 as well).</p>
                        <p>Cancellara left a select group 210 kilometers into the race, at the 3-kilometer long Mons-en-Pévèle cobbled sector. <a href="../../photo-galleries/rider-gallery/boonen-tom.html">Boonen</a> was at the back of the small group when Cancellara attacked, but I can't see how it would have mattered. Before easing up Cancellara had hammered out a 3-minute lead. The Swiss champion is having a period of grace that few sportsmen ever get to enjoy. Chapeau.</p>
