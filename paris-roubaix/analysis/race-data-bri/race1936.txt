														<h1>1936 Paris - Roubaix </h1>
														<h2>37th edition: Sunday, April 12, 1936</h2>
													</div>
												<!-- InstanceEndEditable -->
  <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="PageText" -->
	<p><a href="paris-roubaix-index.html">Paris-Roubaix podium history</a> | <a href="pr1937.html">1937 edition</a> | <a href="pr1935.html">1935 edition </a></p>
												<p>262 km. 155 starters and 40 classified finishers. </p>
												<p>Rainy. Not having a good sprint, three-time winner Gaston Rebry needed to wear down the group of thirteen leaders. He reduced them to three: Georges Speicher, Rimain Maes and himself, of whom Speicher had the best finish. On the racetrack  Maes astonished all by crossing the line first. But the judges saw it differently and unjustly awarded the race to Georges Speicher. We know the decision to be wrong because a photo has survived clearly showing Maes winning the sprint.</p>
												<p><img src="../../images-all/classics-images/paris-roubaix-hist/1936-PR-speicher.jpg" width="550" height="448" alt="1936 Paris-Roubaix finish" /></p>
												<p class="cap">Romain Maes (left) crosses the line before Speicher (right), but the judges didn't see it that way.</p>
