														<h1><b>2004 Paris - Roubaix </b></h1>
														<h3><b>102nd edition: Sunday, April 11, 2004</b></h3>
														<h3>Complete results, photos and race synopsis</h3>
													</div>
												<!-- InstanceEndEditable -->
  <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="PageText" -->
												<p>261 km. 51.1 kilometers of cobbles divided into 26 sections</p>
												<p><a href="paris-roubaix-index.html">Podium History of Paris-Roubaix</a> | <a href="pr2003.html">2003 edition</a> | <a href="pr2005.html">2005 edition</a></p>
												<p><a href="#results">Complete results</a> | <a href="#photos">photos</a></p>
												<p>Weather in Paris at noon: Dry, mostly sunny. 9C (48F) with the wind NNE 11 mph (18 kph). Since the race heads north, this is a headwind.</p>
												<p><strong>The Race:</strong> The winning group emerged from the last  tough cobbled section, Carrefour de l'Arbre,  at km 245. Both <a href="../../photo-galleries/rider-gallery/van-petegem-peter.html">Peter Van Petegem</a> and <a href="../../photo-galleries/rider-gallery/museeuw-johan.html">Johan Museeuw</a> were in that group, but got flats near the end of the race, ruining their chances. They crossed the finish line in the Roubaix Velodrome arm in arm (see picture at bottom of this page). Those flats left four riders, Magnus Backstedt, Tristan Hoffman, Roger Hammond and Fabian Cancellara to contend for victory. Cancellara led into the velodrome. Backstedt saw an opening and zoomed down the banking into the inside and won the biggest race of his career.</p>
